Statement of Need

Ourmarginal societies suffer from innate poverty. Orphans and vulnerablechildren, persons with a disability, widows, the elderly and alsoyoung rural women are the hardest hit. These individuals and groupslive on less than a dollar a day.

Thereare massive food shortages in the communities. They are deprived froma decent meal hence prone to malnutrition diseases. Most of them liveon two light meals per day to keep them alive. A balanced diet inthis context is usually a nightmare. For those in elderly homes andorphanages food supply is a great challenge. Most communities havelittle to none good floors of income to sustain them hence the needto establish some projects.

Educationis a cornerstone to human kind however poverty has denied our belovedcommunities. Most marginalised communities are not able to attendschool due to lack of incomes to meet the school fees demand. A highrate of dropouts is experienced. A majority of these will contributeto a low literacy rate and create vulnerable communities.

Themajority of the population in context has no decent shelter. It isusually a situation of overpopulation or exposure to harsh weatherand failure to afford basic utilities. These need a good income andsome projects would be best suited a lasting solution.

Medicalbills have posed a life threatening situation. In the face of thepandemics the poor cannot survive the costs even the medical subsidycannot reach far to them. HIV/AIDS, malaria corona virus and cancerto name a few have robed our loved ones due to poverty.

Throughincome generating projects at both individual and institutionallevels more income can be generated to sustain a life. Communitieshave either tried and gave up or contemplated starting incomegenerating projects to address their basic needs. GCET is there tobridge the knowledge gap and empower communities throughentrepreneurship. Projects will be from all sectors of the economywith a major thrust in agriculture.
